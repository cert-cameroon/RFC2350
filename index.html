3. Charter

3.1 Mission statement

CERT-CM mission is to provide high quality monitoring and incident management services for its constituency along with contributing to the security community by sharing information, experience and knowledge.

3.2 Constituency

Constituency consists of the institutions/companies who sign an agreement to use CERT-CM security management services. CERT CM constituency is based on confidential agreements.

3.3 Sponsorship and/or affiliation

CERT-CM is an initiative of , Security Department (http://www./).

3.4 Authority

CERT-CM operates under the auspices of, and with authority delegated by (official documentation). The detailed scope of authority towards the constituency is included in confidential agreements.

4. Policies

4.1 Co-operation, interaction and disclosure of information

CERT-CM cooperates with:

  • Abuse Forum (Cameroon Forum for Incident Response Teams)
  • ANTIC (National Cybersecurity Center in Cameroon)
  • CmNOG (The Community for Telecom Professionals)

CERT CM is listed within , pursuing for accreditation.

All secure data when transmitted to its partners is encrypted or/and transmitted over secure environment.

4.2 Communication and authentication

CERT CM uses PGP as well as TLP-Traffic Light Protocol:
RED - personal for named recipients only
AMBER - limited distribution
GREEN - community wide
WHITE - unlimited, may be distributed freely, without restriction.

5. Services

Reactive services:

  • alerts and warnings
  • artifact analysis
  • artifact response
  • forensic analysis
  • incident analysis
  • incident response
  • incident response support
  • vulnerability analysis
  • vulnerability response

Proactive services:

  • announcements
  • intrusion detection services
  • security-related information dissemination
  • technology watch

Security quality management services:
  • awareness building
  • education/training
  • risk analysis
  • security consulting
